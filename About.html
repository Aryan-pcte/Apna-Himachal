History


  • Himachal Pradesh originated as Dev Bhoomi (Land of the Gods).



  • Ancient scriptures describe it as a meditation land for sages.
  • The Himalayan terrain (Shivaliks) protected the region from frequent invasions.


  • Small independent hill kingdoms gradually developed over time.

  • Himachal Pradesh became a full-fledged Indian state on January 25, 1971, making it India's 18th state.

Cultural Tribes

  • Predominantly inhabit Chamba, Kangra, and Bharmaur regions.

  • Traditionally semi-nomadic pastoralists, rearing sheep and goats.

  • Seasonal migration between high-altitude pastures and lower valleys.

  • Distinct cultural identity with traditional attire like chola, dora, and woolen caps.

  • Deeply religious, with strong devotion to Lord Shiva and local deities.

  • Mainly found in Kangra, Una, and parts of Chamba districts.

  • Primarily nomadic or semi-nomadic cattle herders, especially buffaloes.

  • Economy based on milk, dairy products, and livestock trading.

  • Speak Gojri language, along with Hindi.

  • Live a close-knit community life, maintaining distinct customs and traditions

Traditional Languages


Hindi

Official language used for administration, education, and communication.

English

Used in higher education, official work, and professional communication.

Pahari

Widely spoken regional language with multiple dialects.

Kangri

Popular dialect in Kangra region with rich folk literature.

Kullui

Spoken in Kullu valley, reflecting local culture and traditions.

Chambeali

Language of Chamba district with historical significance.


Traditional Wearables

    HIMACHALI CAP

  • Traditional headgear of Himachal Pradesh.
  • Worn by men during festivals and ceremonies.
  • Made of wool with colorful band.
  • Represents regional identity.

    CHOLA–DORA

  • Traditional women’s attire.
  • Chola is a long gown.
  • Dora is a waist belt.
  • Ideal for cold climate.

    GHAGRI–KURTI

  • Traditional women’s dress.
  • Popular in hill regions.
  • Worn during cultural events.
  • Often brightly colored.
